What This Is

Not a course. A practical introduction to the Brussels ecosystem.

IteraLab is designed to make the Brussels ecosystem understandable both as a decision-making environment and as a professional landscape.

Brussels is one of the world's most important centres for public policy, regulation, interest representation, institutional communication, European journalism, EU law and access to EU funds. Yet for those looking at it from the outside, it can appear complex, competitive and opaque.

IteraLab helps participants decode that system in practice. You will understand how roles are structured across EU institutions, consultancies, trade associations, NGOs, companies, law firms, public-affairs teams, media outlets and organisations working on EU funding.

You will also explore how careers actually begin: which profiles are in demand, how recruitment works, which skills matter, and how to make choices that are coherent with the real dynamics of the Brussels market.

This is not limited to classroom learning. It is an applied experience built around direct contact with professionals, practical orientation, institutional visits, networking and the foundations of a long-term community.

Our Lecturers

Learn from the people who work inside the European ecosystem.

IteraLab lecturers are EU professionals with direct experience in the institutions, NGOs, European associations, public-affairs agencies, EU journalism, EU funding, law, advocacy, strategic communications, policy analysis and the private sector.

They bring participants beyond formal descriptions of European careers and into the reality of how Brussels works: how decisions are prepared, how organisations influence policy, how information circulates, how opportunities emerge, and how young professionals can build credible paths into the ecosystem.

Our speakers include professionals from:

  • EU institutions and former institutional officials
  • NGOs and civil-society organisations
  • European associations and interest-representation bodies
  • Public-affairs, advocacy and lobbying agencies
  • EU journalism and Brussels-based media
  • EU funding and project-development specialists
  • Law firms, consultancies and strategic communications experts
  • Private companies and in-house public-policy teams

Why It Matters

Proximity changes trajectories.

Understanding Europe requires more than institutional theory. It requires understanding how decisions are made in practice, how professional roles are defined, how organisations interact, and how careers begin and develop within that environment.

For many students and young professionals, Brussels remains difficult to read from the outside. Opportunities exist, but they are not always visible. Career paths are rarely linear. The most useful information is often not found in university programmes, institutional websites or formal job descriptions.

IteraLab exists to close that gap.

By giving participants early access to the right knowledge, the right professionals and the right interpretation of the ecosystem, IteraLab helps them move with greater awareness, method, credibility and confidence.

The value of the experience is not only in the programme content. It lies in direct access to people, places, experiences and information that normally take years to understand — and in the creation of a lasting bridge between talent, ambition and opportunity.
